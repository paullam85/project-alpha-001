# Mission 003: Market Validation System for Roof AI

## Goal

Validate whether real U.S. homeowners are willing to visit the Roof AI MVP and submit their email + ZIP code for an AI-style roof decision report.

Primary MVP URL:

```txt
https://paullam85.github.io/project-alpha-001/
```

## Validation Thresholds

- Red: 100 visitors, 0 leads
- Yellow: 100 visitors, 1-4 leads
- Green: 100 visitors, 5+ leads

## Ground Rules

- Do not buy ads.
- Do not scrape users.
- Do not spam communities.
- Respect subreddit, Facebook group, and Nextdoor rules.
- Lead with homeowner research and useful discussion.
- Share the link only when allowed or when someone asks.
- Track objections as carefully as leads.

## Comment Reply Templates

1. When someone asks for the tool:
Thanks for asking. Here is the prototype: https://paullam85.github.io/project-alpha-001/ It is meant for planning before contractor quotes, not as a replacement for inspection. I would genuinely appreciate feedback on whether the report feels useful.

2. When someone shares a roof cost:
That is helpful context, thank you. Do you remember the rough roof size, roof type, and whether there were leaks or decking repairs? Those seem to be the factors that make estimates swing a lot.

3. When someone says online estimates are inaccurate:
I agree they can be misleading if they pretend to be exact. The goal here is not an exact quote; it is a planning range and repair/replace guidance before someone talks to contractors. What would make that feel more honest or useful?

4. When someone asks if this is a roofing company:
No, this is not a roofing company. It is a small validation project to learn whether homeowners want a planning report before getting quotes. No contractor database is connected right now.

5. When a moderator asks whether this is promotional:
Thanks for checking. This is a research/validation post for a free homeowner planning tool, not a paid service or contractor promotion. I am happy to remove the link or keep discussion-only if that better fits the group rules.

6. When someone asks about privacy:
The current form asks for email and ZIP to unlock the report. It does not ask for address or phone number. The goal is to understand whether homeowners are willing to request a report, not to sell personal details.

7. When someone says they already have contractor quotes:
That is exactly the kind of situation I am trying to understand. Would a planning report have helped before you got quotes, or was the contractor inspection the only thing that mattered?

8. When someone asks about insurance:
Insurance can change the decision a lot, especially after storm damage. The prototype is focused on planning, not claim advice. I am tracking insurance as a common reason homeowners need more guidance.

9. When someone says roof type matters:
Agreed. The prototype includes roof type because asphalt, metal, tile, and flat roofs can change cost significantly. Are there other factors you think are essential without making the form too long?

10. When someone gives negative feedback:
That is useful, thank you. I would rather learn that early. Was the issue trust, estimate accuracy, wording, privacy, or the idea itself?

## Follow-Up Reply Templates

### After Someone Tries The Tool

Thanks for trying it. Quick question: did the report feel useful enough to justify entering email + ZIP, or did that feel like too much friction?

### After Someone Comments But Does Not Click

Appreciate the perspective. If you were planning roof work, what would be the one thing you would want answered before calling contractors?

### After Someone Mentions Trust

That makes sense. I am trying to make the tool clear that it is a planning estimate, not a quote. Would stronger disclaimers, more cost factors, or no email gate improve trust?

### After Someone Mentions Price Accuracy

Good point. Roof cost changes a lot with local labor, decking, pitch, and materials. The current goal is to give a useful planning range. What range width would feel honest rather than fake-precise?

### After Someone Objects To Email Capture

That is helpful feedback. The email gate is part of the validation test, but if it feels too early or too aggressive, that is important to know. Would you prefer seeing the full report first and email only for saving it?
